about_me

who i am

I am a student at the Delft University of Technology, an all-round web developer, and also a massive robotics enthusiast.

what i do

  1. Currently I am a student at the Delft University of Technology where I study computer science and engineering.
  2. Since 2017 I've had a part-time job as a web developer at HappyFlow, the Hague.
  3. I have a minor in robotics. During this minor we built a life-size interactie robotic guide named IONA for the Sience Centre, Delft.
  4. In the past I have competed in the RoboCupJunior national and world championships, of which i won the former.
  5. Now I am on the board that organises this national championship.
  6. Toghether with a team consisting of university personnel and students I am working on a robotics platform to help teach highschoolers the art of programming.
  7. I am a member of the Robotics Study Association (RSA) that aims to connect students in the robotics community to the industry.

quick words

I like to play around with provided assets like images and documents. However, I do not own licenses to the Microsoft and/or Adobe software that is often required for these kinds of things. FOSS software is often enough for me. I wish more assets were provided in non-proprietary file formats.

I give funny looks to people who believe that modern is always better. There is a time and place for everything, but when your web page takes a full minute to load you must reassess your priorities.

Tabs are always better than spaces, except for aligning stuff. Linux kernel style is the only proper way to place brackets. Vim is better than Emacs, but only because no one except Richard bothers with the steep learning curve. I prefer Gnome over KDE. You can be an expert on any programming language and still dislike said language, no shame.
